Fish

Breathe through gills, and some can change sex or color to adapt to the environment.

Crab

Crustaceans with strong claws, they move sideways and can regenerate lost legs or claws.

Dolphin

Very intelligent, they use echolocation to find food and communicate. Some show complex social behaviors.

Eel

Long, snake-like fish, some can generate electric shocks. They migrate long distances to reproduce.

Jellyfish

Simple animals without a brain or heart. Their bodies are 95% water, and they have stinging cells for hunting and defense.

Lobster

Marine crustaceans with strong claws, live on the sea floor, and some can live over 50 years.

Octopus

Very smart, with 8 arms, 3 hearts, and blue blood. They can change color and texture to hide, and some can learn.

Sea Horse

Small fish with a horse-shaped head. They swim upright and use their tails to hold onto plants or coral. Uniquely, the male carries the eggs in a special pouch until they hatch.

Seal

Marine mammals that live both in water and on land. They have a thick layer of fat for cold protection and can dive for several minutes.

Sea Turtle

Reptiles that live in the sea but lay eggs on beaches. They always return to their birthplace.

Sea Urchin Purple

Round animals covered in spines, live on rocks, and have strong teeth to grind food.

Shark

Powerful predators with a cartilaginous skeleton and regenerating teeth. Some can detect a single drop of blood in millions of liters of water.

Squid

Has 10 arms, releases ink to escape predators, and has large eyes for excellent deep-sea vision.

Starfish

Has no brain, moves using water-filled tubes, and can regenerate lost arms or even its whole body.

Whale

The largest marine mammals, they breathe air through a blowhole. Some species reach 30 meters and weigh over 150 tons. They mainly eat krill or small fish.
