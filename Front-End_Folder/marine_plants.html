Pink Branching

Coral with branching tree-like shapes. Often grows in shallow reefs and provides shelter for many small fish. Sensitive to temperature changes and bleaching.

Red Branching

Bright red coral with dense branches. Known for fast growth in warm clear waters. Plays a major role in building reef structures.

White Staghorn-Like

Hard coral with sharp, antler-shaped branches. Very vulnerable to bleaching. Helps stabilize reefs by forming large complex colonies.

Ivory Cluster

Rounded coral with compact, short branches. Strong wave-resistant species commonly found near reef edges. Important for protecting coastlines.

Blue-Pink Fusion

Color-gradient branching coral. Some species change color based on lighting and algae inside them. Attracts many reef fish.

Red Lettuce-Like

Soft, folded coral that looks like flower petals. Good at filtering water and removing small particles. Often grows in low-light areas.

Deep Red Flower

Flat coral with wide, wavy folds. Known for expanding at night to catch plankton. Can glow under UV light.

Blue Branching

Vivid blue coral with thick branches. Color comes from special algae and proteins. Requires very clean, oxygen-rich water.

Peach Brain-Like

Rounded coral with maze-like folds. Slow-growing and can live for hundreds of years. Stores carbon inside its skeleton.

Purple Bumpy

Cluster coral with rounded tips. Hardy and adaptable to different currents. Provides hiding spaces for small crustaceans.

Purple Folded

Large coral with deep folds. Expands and contracts its tissue to feed. Helps recycle nutrients in reef systems.

Pink Mushroom-Style

Looks like stacked mushroom caps. Often found in calm lagoons. Can reproduce by dropping small clones.

Blue Honeycomb

Coral with many small holes forming a dome shape. Excellent at filtering water and catching plankton. Known for strong natural skeleton.

Lavender Branch

Tall coral with finger-shaped arms. Resistant to strong waves. Plays a role in shading young corals and algae.

Green Cluster

Rounded coral with thick, short branches. Stores water inside its tissues to survive harsh conditions. Grows in medium-depth reefs.
